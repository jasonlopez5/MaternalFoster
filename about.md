## Intended Audience
- My app's intended audience are pregnant women. More specifically, those in low-income neighborhoods who don't have
access to or aren't aware of reliable information that is out there for them. This can be due to financial issues, lack of opportunites and resources,
not having a good support system in place for them such as having close relationships to family and/or friends, etc. Because of this, these pregnant women
are having to go through a very difficult 9 month experience where they are having to figure stuff out along the way,
without really knowing if what they are doing or experiencing is right or not.

## Tech Highlights
- The APIs I used in this project were the CDC Content Services API and the MyHealthfinder API.
- In my database, I store the pregnancy week ranges for my Week-By-Week development page. The pregnancyWeekRanges collection contains many weeks range documents such as Weeks 1-4, 5-8, etc.
  Each document stores babyDevelopment, id, motherChanges, order, range, sources, summary, tips, title, and trimester as fields.
- Something a bit annoying was that I couldn't find any free API that provided me with a week by week development information.
  I did find one API for this, but I would have had to pay for it and I didn't want to do that. So, I had to find a reliable, medically reviewed source that provided me with this information and summarize it myself.
- Some things I found cool was mostly working on the UI of the app, I really had fun designing it and thinking about how I could improve my app.
